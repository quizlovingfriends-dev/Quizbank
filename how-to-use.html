METHOD.

HOW_TO_ACTUALLY_GET_GOOD_AT_QUIZZING // V1

Quizzing is not about facts.

It looks like it is. A quiz question feels isolated. Saffron. Brian Lara. The largest desert in Africa. Most people learn quiz answers the same way — one fact at a time, hoping enough stick.

That doesn't scale. There are too many possible questions and not enough hours in your life to memorize each one.

Quizzing is actually about lists.

DEFINITION

Every quiz answer is one item from a larger list that lives in your head. The list is the actual knowledge. The fact is just a pointer into it.

The saffron example.

Say you're asked: From which part of the plant is saffron derived?

The "fact answer" is: the dried stigmas of the Crocus sativus flower.

But the useful answer — the one that helps you with 20 future questions — is the list this fact belongs to:

LIST_034 // SPICES_BY_PLANT_PART
  • SAFFRON ───── stigmas (Crocus sativus)
  • CINNAMON ──── inner bark
  • CLOVES ────── dried flower buds
  • TURMERIC ──── rhizome
  • GINGER ────── rhizome
  • VANILLA ───── seed pod
  • PEPPER ────── dried berries
  • CARDAMOM ──── seed pods
  • NUTMEG ────── seed kernel
  • MACE ──────── seed coat (of nutmeg)

Once you know this list, you can also answer:

  • "What part of the plant is cinnamon?"
  • "Which spices come from rhizomes?"
  • "What is mace, and what is it related to?"
  • "Which seed produces two different spices?"

One memorized list. Four guaranteed correct answers. Infinite lateral questions you can now reason your way through.

So here's how to use QuizVault.

STEP_01

Answer the question.

Open practice mode. Type your answer. Get it right or wrong. That's the easy part.

STEP_02

Read the funda.

The funda is the context that turns a fact into a story. It's how your brain actually holds on to information. Don't skip it. Read it slowly.

STEP_03

Study the related lists.

Under every answer, you'll see 1-3 "RELATED LISTS." These are the actual mental maps you should build. Star the ones that feel important. Come back to them in your WIKI.

STEP_04

Practice retrieval, not recognition.

Re-reading is not studying. Recalling is studying. The site sends questions you got wrong back at you automatically (SM-2 spaced repetition) — let it. Don't peek.

STEP_05

Track your weak topics.

Your STATS page shows you which topics you're consistently missing. Practice those. Not the ones you already know.

The promise.

Do this for three months. Twenty minutes a day. You will not become a champion. But you'll be measurably better than ninety percent of people who say they're "into quizzing." You'll have real mental maps instead of trivia debris. You'll start seeing connections between questions.

That's it. That's the method.
